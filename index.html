Learn literary terms.

Using quotes from Romeo and Juliet, A Christmas Carol, and more, this question bank helps you learn and identify literary terms throughout the works you study.

Download for your platform Download for other platforms

Features

Passages from all your texts

Find examples from a multitude of Grade 9 English texts, from classics like Romeo and Juliet and A Christmas Carol to more modern selections like A Taste of Melon and Lamb to the Slaughter.

All kinds of quotes with all kinds of literary devices

With the sizeable number of quotes, you'll be sure to find examples of your most (and least) favourite literary devices. Similes and symbolism and synecdoche, oh my!

Clues to help when you're stuck

Sometimes there are literary terms that just harder to identify. Even Ms. Bigham gets confused between synecdoche and metonymy sometimes! When that happens, clues and explanations will be there to help you out.

More examples for the same literary device

If you want to see more examples of one type of literary device, most of the quotes are related to other quotes that have the same or similar literary device. Type in the quote number and go!

The full canonical list of literary devices

Included is a PDF file containing the entire list of literary devices that you should know by the end of ninth grade. Use it for reference, or print it out as a backup copy!
